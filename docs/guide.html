What you now own

1. A Telegram briefing service. Every new upload from your channels arrives as a card: The Gist — a one-line takeaway, an Economist-style summary, the key data points, the concepts worth learning, and why it matters — 1–2 minutes to read instead of 30 minutes to watch. Videos without captions work too; the system watches the video itself.

2. Living knowledge maps. Each channel has a mind map of its worldview that grows with every video. The updated map arrives as a picture after each briefing.

3. A reading room. A private website of your own where every briefing is beautifully archived — scroll the timeline, expand any card, revisit any idea:

4. A research library. Everything is also saved to a Google Drive folder as documents, ready to load into NotebookLM whenever you want to go deep on a channel.

How to drive it (in Telegram)

/add @handleFollow a channel — paste the YouTube handle or the channel URL. The bot will ask how much history to digest: full history, last 30 days, 6 months, 12 months, or new videos only.
/remove 2Unfollow (use the number from /list). Past briefings are kept.
/listSee what you're following.
/statusQueue and today's digest count.
/helpAll commands.

One quirk to know: the system wakes up on a schedule rather than living on a server, so replies to commands can take five minutes to arrive. New-video briefings come automatically — usually within the hour of upload.

Going deep with NotebookLM

  1. Open notebooklm.google.com and create a notebook.
  2. Add source → Google Drive → pick "_NotebookLM Pack — [channel]" from your briefings folder. That single doc contains every briefing from that channel and is refreshed automatically.
  3. In the Drive folder there's a doc called "_START HERE — NotebookLM Prompt" — copy the prompt inside it into NotebookLM's chat and it will synthesize the channel's entire worldview for you: recurring theses, frameworks, contradictions, open questions. If you are not sure how, just ask any AI — it will walk you through.
