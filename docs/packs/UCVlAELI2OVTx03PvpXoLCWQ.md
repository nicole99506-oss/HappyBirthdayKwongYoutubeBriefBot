# Iran, China, Fascism, and Boycotting FIFA | Ajamu Baraka & Dimitri Lascaris

**Channel:** BettBeat Media  
**Published:** 2026-07-19  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Phvnx5x56y8

> **The Gist** — BRICS nations, driven by capitalist structures and elite interests, often prioritize global economic stability over ideological anti-imperialism, as seen in China's oil market interventions.

## Summary

This segment analyzes the geopolitical friction between China's market-stabilizing actions and Iran's strategic use of oil as an economic weapon against Western hegemony. Following the outbreak of conflict, China significantly reduced its oil imports, which, alongside massive releases from the US Strategic Petroleum Reserve, kept global oil prices unexpectedly low. While some analysts attribute this to China's concern for the Global South or tactical hedging to avoid extreme Western retaliation, the speakers argue that these actions expose the limits of ideological solidarity within the anti-imperialist bloc.

Ajamu and Dimitri Lascaris contend that observers must evaluate these states by their material outcomes rather than rhetorical stances. They highlight a persistent "naivety" in Russian and Chinese foreign policy, which often pursues "peaceful coexistence" with a Western imperialist system that is fundamentally committed to maintaining its global hegemony. This naivety is structurally linked to the domestic political economies of BRICS nations.

Ultimately, the discussion concludes that BRICS members—including China, Russia, India, and Brazil—are fundamentally capitalist societies ruled by economic elites. While they challenge US unilateralism, their state actions are heavily influenced by domestic oligarchs and the preservation of global capitalist markets, meaning they cannot be viewed as pure or reliable anti-imperialist actors.

## Highlights

- China's dramatic reduction in oil imports after February 28, combined with US strategic reserve releases, prevented a predicted spike in global oil prices.
- Analysts suggest China's market stabilization may be a "slow boil" strategy to avoid triggering extreme, potentially nuclear, reactions from the US and Israel.
- The speakers critique Russian and Chinese foreign policy for a "strange naivety" in expecting rational, peaceful coexistence with Western imperialism.
- BRICS nations are characterized not as ideological saviors, but as capitalist states whose foreign policies are compromised by domestic billionaire elites.
- Iran's internal political landscape remains divided between neoliberal moderates seeking Western integration and radicals pushing for hardline resistance.

## Key Concepts

- **Materialist Analysis** — Evaluating geopolitical actors based on the physical and economic consequences of their actions rather than their stated ideological intentions.
- **BRICS Capitalism** — The reality that major emerging economies operate as capitalist systems with powerful oligarchies, limiting their commitment to genuine global anti-imperialism.
- **Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR)** — Government-controlled fuel stocks used to manipulate market supply and suppress oil prices during geopolitical crises.

## Why It Matters

This analysis demystifies the BRICS bloc, showing that global multipolarity is shaped more by competing capitalist interests and elite preservation than by cohesive anti-imperialist ideology.
